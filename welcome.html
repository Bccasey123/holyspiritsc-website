From Our Pastor

A Warm Welcome from Fr. Stephen

Dear friends,

It is my great joy to welcome you to Holy Spirit Catholic Church. Whether you are joining us for the first time, returning to the Church after some time away, or exploring the Catholic faith with an open and curious heart — know that you are truly, warmly welcome here.

This parish is a community of people who believe that God has more in store for each of us than we can ask or imagine. We are not perfect people — far from it — but we are people who are striving together to encounter the living God, to grow in faith, and to love our neighbors as Christ has loved us.

It is my prayer that you will find in Holy Spirit a community where you belong, where your faith can deepen and come alive, and where the transformative power of Christ working through the Holy Spirit becomes real in your life. I invite you to join us for Mass each week, to take a next step into community, and to let us know how we can support you on your journey.

God bless you. We are glad you are here.

Father Stephen Dyas

Pastor, Holy Spirit Catholic Church

You Are Always Welcome Back

Have You Been Away from the Church?

If you have been away from the Catholic Church for a while, please know this: you are always welcome back. No matter how long it has been, or what has kept you away, there is no judgment here — only a warm and open door.

Many people find their way back to the Church through a gradual process — beginning by simply attending Mass, then perhaps celebrating the Sacrament of Reconciliation, and reconnecting with a community of faith at their own pace. We understand that returning can feel daunting. We want to make it as simple and as welcoming as possible.

We would be honored to walk with you. If you would like to speak with Fr. Stephen or a member of our staff, please reach out — there is no pressure, just an open conversation.

Thinking About Becoming Catholic?

The OCIA Journey

Do you have questions about Jesus, Christianity, or the Catholic faith? Would you like to receive the sacraments? You are in the right place.

OCIA — the Order of Christian Initiation of Adults (formerly known as RCIA) — is not just a class. It is a chance to allow God to speak to you personally, to encounter his love for you, and to discern whether the Catholic Church is where you are being called. You are not required to become Catholic to participate. You are simply invited to come and see.

Our OCIA process is a welcoming, unhurried journey through Scripture, Tradition, and the sacraments — accompanied by a community of people who have asked the same questions you are asking.

OCIA is for you if:

  • You have never been baptized and are drawn to the Christian faith
  • You were baptized in another Christian tradition and feel called to become Catholic
  • You were baptized Catholic but never completed your sacraments
  • You simply want to learn more about what Catholics believe

We Have Answers

Common Questions

Where is the church located?

We are located at 3871 Betsy Kerrison Pkwy, Johns Island, SC 29455. We are on Johns Island, just minutes from Kiawah Island and a short drive from West Ashley and James Island.

What are the Mass times?

Weekend Mass is Saturday at 5:30 pm, Sunday at 9:00 am and 11:00 am, and Sunday in Spanish at 1:00 pm. Daily Mass is Monday–Friday at 8:30 am, and Saturday at 9:00 am in the Chapel. See our full Mass schedule.

How do I register as a parishioner?

You can register online, stop by the parish office during office hours (Monday–Thursday, 8:30 am–4:00 pm), or call us at (843) 768-0357. We would love to welcome you officially.

How do I become Catholic?

The path to becoming Catholic is through our OCIA process (Order of Christian Initiation of Adults, formerly called RCIA). It is a welcoming, unhurried journey — not just a class. We have interest gatherings in June and November. Learn more about OCIA.

What if I have been away from the Church for a while?

You are always welcome back. Many people return to the Church gradually — beginning with Mass, then perhaps the Sacrament of Reconciliation. There is no judgment here. If you would like to talk to someone, please reach out to us and we will connect you with Fr. Stephen or a member of our team.

How do I get involved?

We have ministries and opportunities to suit every gift, schedule, and stage of life. The best way to start is to browse our ministries page or simply contact the parish office and tell us a little about yourself — we will help you find where you fit.

Are children welcome at Mass?

Absolutely — children are always welcome at Mass. Families with young children often find our 9:00 am Sunday Mass a good fit. We also offer a Children's Liturgy of the Word at select Masses during the school year. Crying rooms are available if needed.
