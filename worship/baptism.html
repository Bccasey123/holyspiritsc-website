The Sacrament of Baptism

Born again in water and the Spirit — welcomed into the family of God.

Baptism is the doorway to the sacramental life — the moment when we are born again as children of God, freed from original sin, and welcomed into the Body of Christ. It is one of the most joyful moments in the life of a family and a parish.

Whether you are preparing to baptize your child or you are an adult seeking Baptism yourself, we are honored to walk with you through this sacred milestone.

For Families

Infant Baptism

Congratulations on the birth of your child! Bringing your baby to Baptism is one of the most loving and faith-filled gifts you can give.

At Holy Spirit, we celebrate infant Baptism with great joy. We ask that parents attend a brief Baptism preparation meeting before your child's Baptism. This is a time to reflect on your role as the primary faith teachers in your child's life — and to receive support and encouragement from our parish community.

What to Expect

Infant Baptism is typically celebrated during or after Sunday Mass, or on a designated Baptism Sunday. During the Rite of Baptism, your child will be anointed with holy oil, water will be poured over their head three times as the priest prays the words of Baptism, and your child will receive a white garment and a baptismal candle — beautiful symbols of their new life in Christ.

Godparents play a meaningful role in your child's life. At least one godparent must be a practicing Catholic who has received the sacraments of Baptism, Confirmation, and Eucharist.

How to Register

Please contact the parish office to schedule your child's Baptism and to register for a preparation meeting. We are happy to assist you and your family.

For Adults

Adult Baptism

Already Baptized in Another Christian Tradition?

The Catholic Church recognizes as valid the Baptism of most Christian denominations — including Baptist, Methodist, Presbyterian, Lutheran, Anglican/Episcopal, and many others — provided the Baptism was performed with water and in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

If you were baptized in another Christian tradition and feel called to become fully Catholic, you do not need to be rebaptized. Instead, you will prepare to receive the Sacraments of Confirmation and the Eucharist. Our OCIA process is the welcoming path for this journey.

Not Yet Baptized?

Unbaptized adults who wish to receive the Sacrament of Baptism are warmly invited to prepare through our OCIA process — the Order of Christian Initiation of Adults. OCIA is not a class but an encounter with God: a journey through Scripture, the sacraments, and community, in which you are invited to discover the Catholic faith at your own pace.

Adults who journey through OCIA are typically received into the Church at the Easter Vigil, receiving Baptism, Confirmation, and First Eucharist in one beautiful celebration.

Common Questions

Baptism FAQ

How soon after birth should I have my child baptized?

The Church encourages parents to baptize their child within the first few weeks of life. If you need more time to prepare, that is perfectly fine — please contact us and we will work with you.

Can a non-Catholic be a godparent?

A baptized Christian from another denomination can serve as a Christian witness alongside a Catholic godparent, but there must be at least one Catholic godparent. The Catholic godparent must have received Baptism, Confirmation, and the Eucharist and be living as a practicing Catholic.

What if one or both parents are not Catholic?

We welcome families in which one or both parents are not Catholic. What the Church asks is that there is a reasonable hope that the child will be raised in the Catholic faith. Please contact us so we can have a conversation about your specific situation.

Do I need to be registered in the parish?

We encourage you to register with Holy Spirit as your home parish. This connects you more fully to our community and allows us to support your family's faith journey in an ongoing way.
