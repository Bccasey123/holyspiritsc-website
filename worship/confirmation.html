The Sacrament of Confirmation

Sealed with the gifts of the Holy Spirit — strengthened for a life of faith.

What Is Confirmation?

Sealed with the Spirit

Confirmation is the sacrament in which a baptized Christian is sealed with the gift of the Holy Spirit and strengthened to live as a full and active member of the Body of Christ.

In Confirmation, the gifts given at Baptism are deepened and strengthened. The seven gifts of the Holy Spirit — wisdom, understanding, counsel, fortitude, knowledge, piety, and fear of the Lord — are poured out anew, equipping the confirmed person to live their faith with courage and joy in the world.

Confirmation is not a graduation from faith — it is a commissioning into it. Those who receive Confirmation are called to be witnesses of Christ in their families, workplaces, and communities.

Confirmation for Teens

Holy Spirit prepares teenagers for the Sacrament of Confirmation through our Youth Ministry and Religious Education programs. Preparation typically spans one to two years and includes sessions, service, retreats, and a personal reflection on the faith.

Contact our Director of Religious Education to learn about the current program schedule and requirements for teen Confirmation candidates.

Confirmation for Adults

Adults who were baptized Catholic but never received Confirmation are warmly encouraged to complete their initiation. Please contact the parish office to discuss your options. In many cases, adults can be confirmed through our OCIA process or in connection with a Diocesan Confirmation ceremony.
