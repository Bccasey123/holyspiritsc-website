Funeral Ministry

In your time of loss, you do not walk alone. Holy Spirit walks with you.

Losing someone you love is one of the most difficult experiences a human being can face. The Catholic Church accompanies the dying and the grieving with prayer, ritual, and community — reminding us that death is not the end, but a passage into the fullness of life in God.

At Holy Spirit, our funeral ministry exists to serve you with compassion, dignity, and practical care during one of the hardest seasons of life. We are honored to walk with you.

Practical Guidance

When a Death Occurs

When a death has occurred, please contact the parish office as soon as you are ready. We will walk you through every step of the process and coordinate with your funeral home to plan a funeral liturgy that honors your loved one with dignity and prayer.

  1. Contact the Parish Office

    Call us at (843) 768-0357 as soon as you are able. We will begin coordinating with you and your family. There is no need to have everything figured out first — just reach out.

  2. Schedule the Funeral Liturgy

    We will work with you and the funeral home to find a date and time. Our staff will help you plan the order of service, readings, and music.

  3. Choose Readings and Music

    Families are invited to participate in selecting the Scripture readings and music. Our Minister of Music can help guide you through the options and choose pieces that are both meaningful and liturgically appropriate.

  4. Meet with the Celebrating Priest or Deacon

    A brief meeting with the celebrating priest or deacon allows your family to share memories and details about your loved one, so that the homily can be personal, meaningful, and truly honoring of the life that was lived.

Funeral Rites

Funeral Liturgy Options

The Catholic Church offers several liturgical options depending on the family's wishes, the circumstances of the death, and available clergy.

Funeral Mass

The fullest expression of Catholic funeral rites is the Funeral Mass — a celebration of the Eucharist in which the deceased is commended to God and the community gathers to pray, grieve, and receive hope. This is the most common and most deeply meaningful option.

Vigil (Wake) Service

A Vigil or Wake service is often held the evening before the Funeral Mass, either at the parish or at the funeral home. It is a time of prayer, remembrance, and comfort — gathering around the body of the deceased in prayerful community.

Rite of Committal

The Rite of Committal takes place at the graveside or mausoleum. It is the final act of the funeral rites, entrusting the body of the deceased to the earth in the hope of resurrection.

A Note on Cremation

Guidelines on Cremation

The Catholic Church permits cremation, provided it is not chosen for reasons that are contrary to Christian belief in the resurrection of the body. The Church asks that cremated remains be treated with the same respect as the body, and that they be buried or interred in a sacred place rather than scattered or kept at home.

A full Funeral Mass can be celebrated with the body present before cremation, or a Mass of Christian Burial can be celebrated with the cremated remains.
