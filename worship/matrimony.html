Marriage & Weddings

Congratulations on your engagement. We are honored to walk with you as you prepare for this beautiful sacrament.

The Sacrament of Marriage

A Covenant of Love

Marriage is not simply a contract between two people — it is a covenant, modeled on the very love that exists within the Trinity: complete, faithful, fruitful, and life-giving.

When a Catholic couple exchanges vows in the Church, they are not simply promising their love to one another. They are becoming a living sign of Christ's love for His Church — a love that holds nothing back, endures all things, and is open to new life. That is a breathtakingly beautiful calling.

The preparation process for Catholic marriage is not a hurdle to clear — it is a gift. It is an opportunity to go deeper with your partner, to explore the foundations of a lifelong commitment, and to invite God into the center of your marriage from the very beginning.

Your Preparation Journey

Steps to Prepare for Marriage

Please contact the parish office at least six months before your desired wedding date to begin the process.

  1. Initial Meeting with the Pastor

    Meet with Fr. Stephen to complete the necessary paperwork, discuss any impediments to marriage, and schedule your wedding date. This is also a time to get to know your pastor and share your vision for your wedding.

  2. Complete the FOCCUS Inventory

    Complete the FOCCUS premarital inventory — a research-based tool that helps couples explore key areas of their relationship — and then meet with a priest or deacon to discuss your results in a supportive, confidential setting.

  3. Attend a Marriage Preparation Retreat

    Attend an approved marriage preparation retreat or program. These weekends are consistently described by couples as one of the most meaningful experiences of their engagement. Details will be provided at your initial meeting.

  4. Meet with the Celebrating Priest or Deacon

    Meet with the priest or deacon who will celebrate your wedding to discuss the order of the ceremony, your chosen Scripture readings, and any special intentions or elements you would like to include.

  5. Meet with the Minister of Music

    Meet with our Minister of Music to choose the music for your wedding Mass or ceremony. Music is a central part of the celebration, and our music director is here to help you create something beautiful and meaningful.

Already Civilly Married?

Convalidation

If you were married civilly and would like to have your marriage recognized and blessed by the Church, this is called convalidation — sometimes called "having your marriage blessed."

Convalidation is a beautiful way to bring your existing marriage fully into the sacramental life of the Church. The process is simpler than a full wedding preparation, and Fr. Stephen would be glad to speak with you about what it involves and how to begin.
