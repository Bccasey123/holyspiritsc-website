The Eucharist

The source and summit of Catholic life — Jesus, truly present in the breaking of the bread.

The Greatest Gift

What Is the Eucharist?

At every Mass, bread and wine are transformed into the Body and Blood of Jesus Christ. This is not a symbol or a memorial — Catholics believe it is the real, true, and substantial presence of Christ Himself.

The Eucharist is the heart of Catholic life. Every other sacrament, every prayer, every act of service flows from and leads back to this encounter at the altar. When we receive Holy Communion, we are not simply remembering Jesus — we are receiving Him.

This mystery — called the Real Presence — is at the center of why Catholics come to Mass every Sunday. We come hungry and we are fed. We come burdened and we are strengthened. We come broken and we are made whole — not by our own effort, but by the One who gives Himself to us completely.

For Children

First Communion

First Communion is a milestone moment in a child's life — the first time they receive the Body and Blood of Jesus in the Eucharist.

At Holy Spirit, children typically prepare for First Communion in second grade, following completion of First Reconciliation. Preparation takes place through our Parish School of Religion (PSR) program, which forms children in the faith, in Scripture, and in the sacramental life of the Church.

First Communion is a joyful family celebration — a moment of grace that children and parents will treasure for a lifetime. We work to make the preparation meaningful and the celebration beautiful.

Preparation Requirements

  • Baptized Catholic
  • Enrolled in our PSR program (or equivalent Catholic school religious education)
  • Typically in second grade or older
  • Have received First Reconciliation
Learn About Our PSR Program Contact Us

Who Can Receive Communion?

At a Catholic Mass, Holy Communion is offered to Catholics who have received their First Communion and are in a state of grace.

Non-Catholics and Catholics who are not in a state of grace are warmly invited to come forward with arms crossed to receive a blessing instead.

Curious About Becoming Catholic?

If you are not Catholic and find yourself drawn to receive Communion, we invite you to explore our OCIA process.
