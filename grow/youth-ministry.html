You Belong Here

Youth Ministry at Holy Spirit — a place where young people encounter Christ, find real community, and discover who they are called to be.

For Middle & High School Students

More Than a Youth Group

Young people are not the future of the Church — they are the Church right now. Our Youth Ministry is a place where teenagers are taken seriously, where questions are welcomed, and where faith is not just taught but experienced.

We gather for faith formation, social events, service opportunities, and retreats throughout the year. Our goal is not to keep young people busy — it is to help them encounter Jesus Christ in a real and personal way, and to give them a community that will walk with them through the most formative years of their lives.

What We Do

Life Together

✝

Faith Formation

Regular gatherings that go beyond surface-level — exploring the big questions of life, Scripture, the teachings of the Church, and what it means to follow Jesus as a teenager in the world today.

🏔

Retreats

Retreats are often where young people experience a turning point in their faith. We offer youth retreats designed to create space for real encounter — away from the noise, and into the presence of God.

🤝

Service

We believe serving others is not optional for a Christian life — it is central. Our youth participate in service projects that connect them to the needs of our parish and our wider community.

🎉

Community & Events

Faith is lived in community. Social events, game nights, service outings, and shared meals are woven throughout our calendar — because real friendship is part of the journey.

Sacramental Preparation

Confirmation

For many young people, Youth Ministry is also where Confirmation preparation happens. This is not a rite of passage to check off — it is an invitation to own your faith as an adult member of the Church.

Confirmation preparation at Holy Spirit is a multi-year process through our PSR program, designed to give young people a deep foundation in what they believe and why — so that receiving the Sacrament is a genuine act of personal commitment rather than a graduation ceremony.
