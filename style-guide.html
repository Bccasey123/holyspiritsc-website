Holy Spirit Catholic Church — Design System

This page documents every color, font, and component we use. Open it alongside any page you're building as a reference. When in doubt, check here first.

01 — Color Palette

5 Core Colors

Every color on the site comes from this set. Each token name starts with --hs-. Use the token names in your CSS — never hardcode hex values. That way, if we ever adjust a shade, it updates everywhere automatically.

1. Navy — Primary Brand

Navy #1E3347 --hs-navy Nav, page headers, footers, headings, dark backgrounds
Navy Mid #2D4E72 --hs-navy-mid Hover states, lighter dark panels
Navy Faint #EBF0F6 --hs-navy-faint Use sparingly — subtle tint only

2. Gold — Liturgical Accent

Gold #B8901F --hs-gold CTAs, active nav indicators, links on hover, Give button
Gold Light #D4AA45 --hs-gold-light Hover on gold, footer headings, eyebrows on dark bg
Gold Faint #F8F0DA --hs-gold-faint Placeholder backgrounds, subtle gold-wash sections

3. Cream — Warm Page Background

Cream #FAF7F1 --hs-cream Default page background. Never use stark white as the page base.
White #FFFFFF --hs-white Cards, modals, form inputs — elements that sit on cream

4. Stone — Warm Gray Replacement

Stone #766556 --hs-stone Secondary body text, captions, supporting copy
Stone Mid #A8967F --hs-stone-mid Borders, dividers, placeholder icon fills
Stone Light #EDE5D9 --hs-stone-light Subtle card borders, section dividers, rule lines

5. Parchment — Deep Warm Background

Parchment #EDE0CC --hs-parchment Alternating section backgrounds, feature callouts
Parchment Dark #D9C9B0 --hs-parchment-dark Borders and dividers used on parchment backgrounds

Text Colors

Ink #1C1510 --hs-ink Primary body text. Warm near-black — not pure black.
Ink Mid #3D3028 --hs-ink-mid Subtext, secondary headings, lead paragraphs
02 — The Gray Problem

Why We Removed Gray

The live holyspiritsc.org site uses cold blue-grays (#6B6B6B for body text, #E8E4DE for backgrounds) inherited from the Diocesan template. These colors read as institutional — the same palette you'd find on a DMV website or an insurance form. Cold gray signals "you're a transaction, not a person."

Every gray in this system has been replaced with a warm stone or parchment equivalent. The visual hierarchy is identical — light, medium, and dark neutrals — but the warmth changes how the page feels to read.

03 — Typography

Font Pairing

Two fonts, used consistently. Lora for all headings (warmth, dignity, classical weight). Source Sans 3 for all body text, UI, and captions (readability, openness, approachability). Never mix these roles — headings stay Lora, body text stays Source Sans 3.

04 — Type Scale

Heading Levels

Each level has a specific semantic role. Don't pick heading levels for their size — pick them for their meaning in the page hierarchy, then let CSS handle the appearance.

Eyebrow Label

H1 — Page Title / Hero Headline

Lead paragraph — the first sentence that introduces a section or page. Slightly larger and more relaxed than body text. Used once per major section.

H2 — Section Heading

Regular body paragraph. Whether you're visiting for the first time or exploring the Catholic faith, you've found a community that's glad you're here. Holy Spirit Parish exists to help you discover the abundant life God has always had in mind for you.

H3 — Subsection / Card Title

Supporting paragraph under an h3. Used inside cards, program descriptions, and content blocks. Stays warm and readable at all sizes.

H4 — Detail Heading

Fine detail copy — schedule times, names, addresses. Caption text sits at 14px and uses --hs-stone for color.

H5 — Label / Overline

Used for category labels and metadata. All caps, widely spaced.

05 — Buttons

Button Styles

Four button variants. Use Primary for the most important action on a page. Use Secondary or Ghost to offer a secondary path. Never use more than two button types side-by-side — it dilutes both.

On Light Backgrounds

Primary — Give Now Secondary — Learn More Ghost Dark — View All Small Primary Small Secondary

On Dark (Navy) Backgrounds

Primary — I'm New Here Ghost — Mass Times Large Primary

When to use which

Primary

Gold — --btn--primary

The single most important action: Give, I'm New, Register, Sign Up. One per section maximum.

Secondary

Navy — .btn--secondary

Supporting action on a light background: Learn More, View Schedule, Read More.

Ghost

White outline — .btn--ghost

Secondary action on dark (navy) backgrounds only. Becomes white on hover.

Ghost Dark

Navy outline — .btn--ghost-dark

Third option on light backgrounds. Use when a filled button feels too heavy.

07 — Voice & Tone

How We Speak

The guiding question for every section of copy: "How does this make a parishioner feel?" Warm and inviting — not institutional or formal. The three audiences we speak to simultaneously: new visitors, current parishioners, and those exploring the faith. Every page should feel safe for all three.

Avoid — Institutional

"The Sacrament of Baptism is the first sacrament of initiation into the Body of Christ, as defined in the Catechism of the Catholic Church (CCC 1213)."

Use — Welcoming

"Baptism is where it all begins — the moment you're welcomed into God's family. Whether it's for your child or yourself, we'd love to walk through it with you."

Avoid — Cold procedure

"To register as a parishioner, submit the Current Registration Form available as a PDF download from the parish office."

Use — Personal invitation

"Ready to make Holy Spirit your parish home? We'd love to have you. Registration takes just a few minutes — call us or stop by the office any weekday."

Avoid — Jargon-first

"The OCIA process consists of four periods: Inquiry, Catechumenate, Purification & Enlightenment, and Mystagogy."

Use — Human first

"Curious about becoming Catholic? You're not alone — and there's no pressure. OCIA is a relaxed, welcoming journey through the faith at your own pace. Classes start June 22."

Avoid — Obligation tone

"Parishioners are expected to support the parish through tithing of time, talent, and treasure."

Use — Abundance tone

"It's actually good for you to give. God's generosity is bigger than any amount you can offer — but sharing what you have opens something in you too."
