Jesus Calls Us to Love and Serve Our Neighbors

Every baptized person is called to ministry. Find your place to make a difference.

Deliver More

We Serve from Joy

Service is central to who we are. Our commitment to serve recognizes that Jesus called us to love one another and to see his face in every person we encounter. When we serve our neighbors, we are not fulfilling an obligation — we are responding to the joy of knowing Christ and sharing that love with the world around us. At Holy Spirit Catholic Church, there is a place for everyone to give of their time and talent in a way that is meaningful and life-giving.

Liturgical Ministries

Ministries that serve at the heart of our worship — helping the whole community encounter Christ in the Mass and the sacraments.

Altar Servers

Altar servers assist the priest at Mass, helping the liturgy unfold with reverence and order. This is one of the great honors of parish life — and it is open to all ages, from children to adults.

Who can serve: ALL AGES — children, teens, and adults. Training is provided.

Volunteer to Serve

Extraordinary Ministers of Holy Communion

Extraordinary Ministers assist with the distribution of the Eucharist at Mass and may bring Communion to those who are homebound or hospitalized. A ministry of profound reverence and pastoral care.

Who can serve: Practicing Catholics in good standing. Training and formation provided.

Learn More

Lectors

Lectors proclaim the Word of God at Mass — a ministry of service to the whole assembly. If you have a love for Scripture and a clear, prayerful presence, this ministry may be your calling.

Who can serve: Adults and teens. Training provided.

Learn More

Ushers & Greeters

Ushers and greeters are often the first face people see when they walk through our doors. This ministry is a powerful form of hospitality — helping everyone feel welcomed, seen, and at home.

Who can serve: Adults and teens. No experience needed.

Sign Up

Music Ministry

Music lifts the heart to God. Our music ministry serves at weekend Masses and special liturgies through choir, instruments, and contemporary praise. Whether you are an experienced musician or just have a heart for worship, come sing with us.

Who can serve: All ages. Auditions not required — just a love of worship.

Learn About Music Ministry

Sacristan

Sacristans prepare the altar and sacred vessels before Mass, ensuring everything is ready for worship. It is a quiet, behind-the-scenes ministry of reverence and care for the sacred.

Who can serve: Adults. Training provided.

Learn More

Faith Formation Ministries

Ministries that form the next generation — and the current one — in the knowledge and love of Jesus Christ.

PSR Catechists

Catechists teach our PSR (Parish School of Religion) classes for children and youth. You do not need a theology degree — you need a genuine faith and a willingness to share it. The reward is extraordinary.

Who can serve: Adults and older teens. Training and materials provided.

Volunteer as a Catechist

Youth Ministry Team

Adult volunteers who walk alongside our middle and high school students — planning events, leading discussions, accompanying retreats, and simply being present to young people navigating faith and life.

Who can serve: Adults. Background check required.

Join the Team

OCIA Team

The OCIA team walks alongside people who are exploring the Catholic faith or preparing to be received into the Church. This ministry of accompaniment — being a sponsor, a team leader, or a welcoming presence — changes lives.

Who can serve: Adults who are practicing Catholics. Training provided.

Outreach & Fellowship

Ministries that extend the love of Christ beyond our parish walls and knit our community together.

Adopt a Family

Each Christmas season, our parish adopts local families in need — providing gifts, food, and essentials for the holidays. This is one of the most tangible ways we live out the Gospel call to care for the poor and vulnerable.

When: Advent / Christmas season. Families and individuals welcome.

Get Involved

Angel Tree

Our Angel Tree ministry provides Christmas gifts to children of incarcerated parents — ensuring that the love of Christ reaches even the most overlooked corners of our community. Take a tag, buy a gift, change a child's Christmas.

When: Advent / Christmas season. Open to all parishioners.

Participate

Local School Support

We partner with local schools to provide support for students and families in need — from supply drives to tutoring to mentorship. Our community extends into the wider Johns Island community, not just our parish campus.

Who can serve: All parishioners. Opportunities vary throughout the year.

Learn More

Bereavement Ministry

Our bereavement team walks alongside families who have lost a loved one — coordinating funeral receptions, providing meals, and offering the simple but profound gift of presence during the hardest season of life.

Who can serve: Adults with a compassionate heart. Please reach out to the parish office to get started.

Get Involved

Hospitality & Fellowship

From post-Mass coffee and donuts to parish events and holiday celebrations — our hospitality team makes Holy Spirit feel like a family. If you love people and love to serve, this is your place.

Who can serve: All ages welcome. Great for families.

Sign Up

Men's & Women's Groups

Our men's and women's groups gather for faith, fellowship, and service throughout the year — including retreats, service projects, and formation events designed specifically for the unique journey of men and women in the Church today.

Open to: All adult men and women in the parish.

Join a Group

Administrative & Operations

Every organization needs people who keep things running. These ministries use practical skills — finance, planning, communication — in service of the whole parish.

Finance Council

The Finance Council advises the pastor on financial matters, stewarding parish resources responsibly and transparently. Members bring skills in accounting, financial planning, or business management.

Who can serve: Adults with financial or business background. Appointment by pastor.

Learn More

Pastoral Council

The Pastoral Council advises the pastor on the overall direction and vision of the parish. Council members bring the voice of the community into parish planning and decision-making.

Who can serve: Practicing Catholic adults. Election and appointment process.

Learn More

Event Volunteers

Parish events — from the annual picnic to OCIA gatherings to special liturgies — depend on volunteers. If you want to help but are not sure where to start, event volunteering is a wonderful entry point.

Who can serve: Anyone. Individual events, no ongoing commitment required.
